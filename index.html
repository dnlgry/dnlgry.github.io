Thinking about counselling?

If you are feeling low, overwhelmed, stuck, disconnected, or caught in patterns that aren't working for you, counselling offers a place to speak openly, make sense of things, and find a way forward.

A counsellor can work collaboratively with you to identify and meet your challenges and goals, at your pace, in whatever way works for you. Counsellors believe that you are the expert in your life. We're not here to diagnoses or label you; or to tell you how you should feel, or what you should do.

A bit about me

Prior to studying integrative psychotherapy in the UK and undertaking postgraduate counselling training in Australia, I had an extensive international career in scientific research, government, consulting, and thought leadership.

My studies span physics, law, public policy, Jungian psychology, and philosophy. I remain committed to personal growth through regular therapy, study, training, and professional supervision.

As a result, I bring relational warmth, therapeutic skill, and broad life experience to my practice, along with a deep interest in how people make sense of themselves and their lives.

Why work with me?

I work with a wide variety of people, including those facing anxiety, depression, relationships, sexuality, addiction, life changes, and questions of meaning or identity. I also do long-term work with people seeking deeper meaning in their lives, grounded in a solid understanding of philosophy and Jungian psychology.

The reality is there's no 'best' approach to counselling. The effectiveness of talking therapy depends on several factors, including the counsellor's skill, experience and training. What’s more important, though, is the connection between the counsellor and the client.

That's why I offer all new clients a free 30-minute video consultation - so we can decide whether we think we'll be able to work well together, without risk or obligation.
